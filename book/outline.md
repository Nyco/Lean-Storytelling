# Lean Storytelling Book Outline

## Overview

This outline provides a structured guide to the Lean Storytelling book, helping readers navigate through the methodology and apply it to their own stories.

## Chapter 1: Lean Storytelling

### Introduction
- What is Lean Storytelling?
- Why "Lean Storytelling"?
- Who It Is Intended For
- How This Works

### Key Characteristics
- Practical set of recipes and templates
- Easy to learn, challenging to master
- Designed for business, product, and feature development
- Applicable from early-stage empathy to delivery
- Helps align teams and clarify the "why"
- Inspired by Lean Canvas, Monomyth, and The Golden Circle
- Rooted in Agile, Lean Startup, Design Thinking, and UX research

### Core Principles
- Start with basic story ingredients
- Add details to thicken the plot
- Polish with the finishing touch
- Continuously test, learn, and adapt
- Refine your story to its essence
- Use the Extension Pack for advanced storytelling

## Chapter 2: Build Your Story

### Basic Story
- **Target**: The hero of your story
- **Problem**: The challenge or antagonism
- **Solution**: Your offering

### Detailed Story
- **Empathy**: What the target sees, feels, hears, and says
- **Consequences**: How the problem impacts the target’s daily life
- **Benefits**: The tangible advantages your solution provides

### Full Story
- **Context**: The environment in which the target operates
- **Why**: The core motivation or guiding principle

## Chapter 3: Extend Your Story

### Extension Pack
- **Challenge**: Pose an open question
- **Quote**: Validate the problem or benefits
- **Alternatives**: Highlight unsatisfactory solutions
- **Competition**: Acknowledge competitors
- **Unfair Advantage**: What makes your solution unique
- **Warnings**: Potential pitfalls or risks
- **Self-Benefits**: How you also benefit
- **Stages in AARRR**: Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Referral, Revenue
- **Call to Action**: What you want your audience to do next
- **Failure**: Share a past failure or setback

### Complex Story
- Use with extreme care
- Target multiple personas
- Address multiple problems
- Blend story arcs

## Chapter 4: Deliver Your Story

### Story Sequencing
- Context

### Formats
- Text: ASCII, PDF, ODF

### Audiences

- Buyers
- Investors

## Chapter 5: Go Beyond Your Story

### Beyond Storytelling
- Test your assumptions
- Refine your value proposition
- Iterate on what is convincing

### Applications
- Describe Backlog, Epics, User Stories
- Test and get feedback on solutions
- Sell products, features, services
- Set the messaging
- Do developer advocacy
- Describe the experience you offer

### Benefits
- Synchronizes and aligns people
- Deliver in various formats
- Spoken, written, visual
